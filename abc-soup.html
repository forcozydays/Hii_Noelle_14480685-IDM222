ABC Soup

Jump to Recipe

Comforting, nourishing, and beautifully simple, Malaysian ABC Soup is a home-style chicken and vegetable soup loved for its natural sweetness and clarity. Made with bone-in chicken, carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, and onion, it’s gently simmered until the broth becomes rich and aromatic. It’s the kind of soup that feels like home — wholesome, light, and deeply satisfying.

Why is it called ABC Soup?

“ABC” stands for Aneka Bahan Campur, which means “mixed ingredients.” It refers to the simple mix of vegetables and protein simmered together. Despite the humble name, this soup is a staple in many Malaysian homes and is often one of the first soups children grow up eating.

What makes the broth taste so naturally sweet?

The sweetness comes entirely from the vegetables. Carrots, onions, and tomatoes slowly release their natural sugars as they simmer. Bone-in chicken adds richness and body, creating a broth that tastes complex without needing much seasoning.

Can you customize ABC Soup?

Absolutely. While the classic version includes carrots, potatoes, and tomatoes, many families add corn on the cob for extra sweetness, red dates for subtle herbal flavor, and cabbage for added texture.

How do you keep the broth clear?

A gentle simmer is key. Avoid a rapid boil, which can cloud the broth. Skim off any foam in the first 10 minutes of cooking, and let everything cook slowly. The result is a beautifully clear, golden soup.

Ingredients

  • Bone-In Chicken (1 lb / 450g)
  • Carrots (2 medium, sliced)
  • Potatoes (2 medium, chunked)
  • Tomatoes (2 medium, quartered)
  • Onion (1 medium, quartered)
  • Garlic (3 cloves, smashed).
  • Water (6 cups / 1.4 liters).
  • White Pepper (0.5 teaspoon).
  • Salt (to taste).

How To

  • Add chicken, water, onion, and garlic to a large pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
  • Skim off any foam.
  • Add carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, and optional corn/red dates.
  • Simmer gently for 45 to 60 minutes until chicken is tender and vegetables are soft.
  • Season with salt and white pepper to taste. Serve warm.
