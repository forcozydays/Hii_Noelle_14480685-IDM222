ABC Soup

Jump to Recipe

Comforting, nourishing, and beautifully simple, Malaysian ABC Soup is a home-style chicken and vegetable soup loved for its natural sweetness and clarity. Made with bone-in chicken, carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, and onion, it’s gently simmered until the broth becomes rich and aromatic. It’s the kind of soup that feels like home — wholesome, light, and deeply satisfying.

“ABC” stands for “Aneka Bahan Campur,” meaning mixed ingredients. It refers to the simple combination of vegetables and chicken simmered together. Slow simmering bone-in chicken releases natural richness, while carrots, tomatoes, and onions add sweetness and subtle acidity. The magic is in the gentle cooking. Though often enjoyed during cooler weather, this soup is light enough for year-round cooking. It’s especially popular as a nourishing family meal. A gentle simmer — not a rolling boil — is key. Skimming off foam early in the cooking process helps maintain that beautifully clear, golden broth.

Ingredients

  • Bone-In Chicken (1 lb / 450g)
  • Carrots (2 medium, sliced)
  • Potatoes (2 medium, chunked)
  • Tomatoes (2 medium, quartered)
  • Onion (1 medium, quartered)
  • Garlic (3 cloves, smashed).
  • Water (6 cups / 1.4 liters).
  • White Pepper (½ teaspoon).
  • Salt (to taste).

How To

  • Add chicken, water, onion, and garlic to a large pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
  • Skim off any foam.
  • Add carrots, potatoes, tomatoes, and optional corn/red dates.
  • Simmer gently for 45–60 minutes until chicken is tender and vegetables are soft.
  • Season with salt and white pepper to taste. Serve warm..
