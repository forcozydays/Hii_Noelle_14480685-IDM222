Banana Bread

A simple, ultra-moist banana bread with a soft, tender crumb and perfectly golden top, this loaf is the ultimate way to use up ripe bananas. It’s quick to make, requires no mixer, and fills your kitchen with the coziest aroma while it bakes. Enjoy it warm with a pat of butter, or elevate it with a swipe of salted butter or a dollop of lightly sweetened whipped cream.

What makes banana bread so moist?

The secret to moist banana bread is very ripe bananas. The darker and spottier the peel, the sweeter and more flavorful the loaf will be. Bananas not only add natural sweetness, but also moisture and tenderness. Using softened butter and room temperature eggs also helps the batter come together smoothly for the best texture.

Can you use frozen bananas?

Yes! Frozen bananas work beautifully in banana bread. Simply thaw them completely and drain off any excess liquid before mashing. They may look a little watery, but once mixed into the batter they add incredible moisture and flavor.

How do you enhance banana flavor?

Aside from using very ripe bananas, a touch of vanilla extract and a small pinch of salt help deepen and balance the flavor. For an extra boost, you can add a sprinkle of cinnamon or swap part of the butter for sour cream or Greek yogurt for a richer, bakery-style finish.

Why did my banana bread sink?

Banana bread can sink if it is underbaked or if there is too much leavening. Be sure to check that your baking soda is fresh and bake until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Avoid opening the oven door too early, as sudden temperature changes can cause the loaf to collapse.

Ingredients

  • Very Ripe Bananas (3 medium): The star of the recipe! The darker and spottier they are, the sweeter and more flavorful your banana bread will be.
  • Unsalted Butter (115g, softened): Adds richness and moisture. It should be very soft, but not melted, so it blends smoothly into the batter.
  • Granulated Sugar (100g) & Brown Sugar (50g): This combination adds sweetness with a subtle caramel depth from the brown sugar.
  • Large Egg (1, room temperature): Binds the ingredients together and gives the loaf structure.
  • Vanilla Extract (1 teaspoon): Deepens and enhances the banana flavor.
  • Baking Soda (1 teaspoon): Helps the banana bread rise and creates that light, fluffy interior — be sure it is fresh for best results.
  • Fine Sea Salt (0.5 teaspoon): Enhances the sweetness and balances the banana flavor beautifully.
  • All-Purpose Flour (190g): Provides structure and gives the loaf its soft, tender crumb. I use standard all-purpose flour for a classic texture.

How To

  • Preheat oven to 350F (175C). Grease or line a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  • In a large bowl, mash the bananas with a fork until mostly smooth.
  • Stir in the melted butter.
  • Mix in the sugars, egg, and vanilla until combined.
  • Sprinkle in the baking soda and salt, then mix.
  • Add the flour and stir just until incorporated.
  • Pour batter into the prepared loaf pan.
  • Bake for 50 to 60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  • Let cool in the pan for 10 to 15 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
