Miso Carbonara

Jump to Recipe

Creamy, savory, and deeply comforting, this miso carbonara is a bold twist on the Italian classic. The silky egg-based sauce coats every strand of pasta, while white miso adds a subtle sweetness and rich umami depth that pairs beautifully with crispy pancetta. It’s simple enough for a weeknight dinner but impressive enough for guests — and best of all, it comes together in under 30 minutes.

What does miso add to carbonara?

White miso adds layered umami and a gentle sweetness that enhances the cheese and egg yolks without overpowering the dish. It deepens the flavor while keeping the sauce balanced and savory.

Do you use cream in carbonara?

Traditional carbonara does not use cream — and neither does this one. The creaminess comes from egg yolks, cheese, and starchy pasta water emulsifying into a silky sauce.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti (12 oz / 340g): The starch helps create a glossy sauce that clings to every strand.
  • Egg Yolks (3 large) + 1 Whole Egg: Create the rich, creamy base..
  • Grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano (1 cup / 90g): Salty, nutty depth.
  • White Miso Paste (1½ tablespoons): Adds umami complexity.
  • Pancetta or Bacon (4 oz / 115g, diced): Crispy, savory contrast.
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced): Aromatic backbone.
  • Black Pepper (1 teaspoon): Add to taste.
  • Salt (for pasta water).

How To

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook spaghetti until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  • In a bowl, whisk egg yolks, whole egg, grated cheese, miso paste, and black pepper until smooth.
  • In a skillet over medium heat, cook pancetta until crisp. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
  • Add drained pasta to the skillet and toss. Remove from heat.
  • Quickly stir in the egg mixture, tossing continuously. Add reserved pasta water a little at a time until glossy and creamy.
  • Serve immediately with extra cheese and cracked pepper.
