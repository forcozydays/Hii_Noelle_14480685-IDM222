Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Jump to Recipe

There’s something magical about brown butter — the way it transforms ordinary cookies into something deeply nutty, rich, and almost caramel-like. These brown butter chocolate chip cookies have crisp golden edges, soft chewy centers, and pools of melted chocolate in every bite. They feel nostalgic, but just a little elevated.

What does brown butter do?

Taking the extra five minutes to brown your butter is an absolute game-changer. The gentle heat toasts the milk solids in the butter, transforming it from a simple fat into a deeply fragrant, nutty, and toffee-like ingredient that completely elevates the entire flavor profile of the cookie, adding an irresistible gourmet touch.

Why chill the dough?

Patience truly pays off with this dough! Resting the cookie dough in the fridge for at least an hour allows the flour to fully hydrate and the vanilla and brown butter flavors to deeply meld. It also solidifies the fat, successfully preventing your cookies from spreading into thin, flat puddles in the hot oven.

The perfect chocolate ratio

For the ultimate bakery-style texture, use a varied mix of high-quality chocolate. Semi-sweet chocolate chips hold their shape well and provide nostalgic, bite-sized bursts of cocoa, while roughly chopped dark chocolate bars melt beautifully, creating gorgeous, irresistible gooey puddles throughout the dough that look and taste absolutely incredible straight out of the oven.

The pan-banging trick

If you love cookies with crispy, crinkled edges and dense, fudgy centers, try the popular pan-banging method. Halfway through baking, carefully lift the baking sheet a few inches and let it drop firmly onto the oven rack. This technique deflates the puffed center of the baking cookie, creating those beautiful, highly sought-after textural ripples.

Ingredients

  • Unsalted Butter (225g): Browned for deep, nutty flavor.
  • Granulated Sugar (100g) & Brown Sugar (200g): This combination adds sweetness with a subtle caramel depth from the brown sugar.
  • Eggs (2 large, room temperature): Binds the ingredients together and gives the cookie structure.
  • Vanilla Extract (2 teaspoons): Deepens and enhances the chocolate flavor.
  • Baking Soda (1 teaspoon)
  • Fine Sea Salt (0.75 teaspoon)
  • All-Purpose Flour (315g): I use standard all-purpose flour for a classic texture.

How To

  • Brown butter in a saucepan until golden and nutty; let cool slightly.
  • Whisk butter with sugars until combined.
  • Add eggs and vanilla, mixing well.
  • Stir in flour, baking soda, and salt. Fold in chocolate.
  • Chill dough 30 to 60 minutes.
  • Scoop onto lined baking sheets and bake at 350F for 10 to 12 minutes.
  • Cool slightly before enjoying.
