Shrimp Egg Soup

Jump to Recipe

Light, comforting, and silky, this shrimp egg soup comes together in minutes and feels both nourishing and satisfying. Delicate egg ribbons float in a savory broth alongside tender shrimp, fresh ginger, and green onions. It’s the kind of simple soup that warms you from the inside out.

There’s something about silky egg ribbons in warm broth that feels instantly soothing. The gentle flavors of ginger and garlic add warmth without overpowering the shrimp’s natural sweetness.

How do you get silky egg ribbons?

Slowly drizzle beaten eggs into gently simmering broth while stirring in one direction. This creates delicate strands rather than clumps.

This soup makes a perfect light lunch, starter course, or cozy evening meal alongside steamed rice. It’s especially lovely when you want something nourishing but not heavy. You can add soft tofu, spinach, or even sweet corn for variation. A drizzle of chili oil at the end adds gentle heat if you like a little spice.

Ingredients

  • Shrimp (8 oz / 225g, peeled and deveined)
  • Eggs (2 large, lightly beaten)
  • Chicken or Seafood Broth (4 cups / 950ml)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
  • Fresh Ginger (1 teaspoon, grated)
  • Soy Sauce (1 tablespoon)
  • Sesame Oil (1 teaspoon)
  • Cornstarch (1 tablespoon + 1 tablespoon water)
  • Green Onions (2 stalks, sliced).
  • Salt & White Pepper (to taste)

How To

  • Bring broth to a gentle simmer.
  • Add garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and shrimp. Cook 2–3 minutes until shrimp are pink.
  • Stir in cornstarch slurry if using.
  • Reduce heat to low and slowly drizzle in beaten eggs while stirring gently.
  • Finish with sesame oil, green onions, and seasoning. Serve immediately.
