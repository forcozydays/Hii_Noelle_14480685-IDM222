Shrimp Egg Soup

Light, comforting, and silky, this shrimp egg soup comes together in minutes and feels both nourishing and satisfying. Delicate egg ribbons float in a savory broth alongside tender shrimp, fresh ginger, and green onions. It’s the kind of simple soup that warms you from the inside out.

A simple comforting soup

There is something so comforting about a soup that comes together quickly but still feels intentional. This shrimp egg soup is exactly that. With just a handful of ingredients, you get a beautifully fragrant broth filled with tender shrimp and silky egg ribbons. It is light, delicate, and quietly impressive — the kind of recipe that feels special without requiring much effort.

The Silky Egg Ribbons

One of my favorite parts of this soup is watching the eggs transform in the broth. As they are slowly drizzled in, they cook instantly into soft, delicate strands that float throughout the soup. It gives the broth a velvety texture and makes each spoonful feel rich while still being light. The key is keeping the heat gentle and letting the eggs set slowly and naturally.

Naturally Balanced and Comforting

The sweetness of the shrimp, the warmth of fresh ginger, and the savoriness of soy sauce all come together in such a balanced way. Nothing is overpowering — every flavor feels soft and rounded. It is the kind of soup you crave when you want something nourishing, soothing, and easy on the stomach.

When you want to clear your fridge

You can add tofu, leafy greens, or even a handful of noodles depending on what you have on hand. It is simple enough for a quiet weeknight dinner but elegant enough to serve as a starter when you are hosting. Once you make it once, it becomes one of those comforting staples you will find yourself returning to often.

Ingredients

  • Shrimp (8 oz / 225g, peeled and deveined)
  • Eggs (2 large, lightly beaten)
  • Chicken or Seafood Broth (4 cups / 950ml)
  • Garlic (2 cloves, minced)
  • Fresh Ginger (1 teaspoon, grated)
  • Soy Sauce (1 tablespoon)
  • Sesame Oil (1 teaspoon)
  • Cornstarch (1 tablespoon + 1 tablespoon water)
  • Green Onions (2 stalks, sliced).
  • Salt & White Pepper (to taste)

How To

  • Bring broth to a gentle simmer.
  • Add garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and shrimp. Cook 2 to 3 minutes until shrimp are pink.
  • Stir in cornstarch slurry if using.
  • Reduce heat to low and slowly drizzle in beaten eggs while stirring gently.
  • Finish with sesame oil, green onions, and seasoning. Serve immediately.
